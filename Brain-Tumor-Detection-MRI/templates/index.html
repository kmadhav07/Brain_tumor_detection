Medical Image Analysis: This system utilizes deep learning to automatically detect the presence of brain tumors in MRI scans. The underlying model was trained on the BRATS 2019 dataset, pushing state-of-the-art accuracy through targeted image preprocessing and transfer learning.
1. Preprocessing Strategy
Instead of feeding raw MRI frames into the network, the backend uses OpenCV to segment the largest connected contour in the image, successfully cropping out skull regions and empty black borders. This focuses the network purely on the brain matter.
2. ResNet-50 Architecture
We use a ResNet-50 model pre-trained on ImageNet. The core convolutional layers act as extreme feature extractors (identifying edges, textures, and ultimately anomalies). We froze the backbone and fine-tuned a custom dense classification head specifically for tumor identification.
3. Training & Validation
The custom head uses strong Dropout layers (p=0.4) to combat overfitting given the dataset size. It was trained using the Adam optimizer with learning rate reduction plateaus, achieving ~99% validation accuracy and F1 score during its original training notebook runs.
